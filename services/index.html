Engineering Pavilion Access

As a ComSSA member you get access to the ground floor of the new Engineering Pavilion in building 215. This building is usually reserved for Bachelor of Engineering students, but—if you're a current Curtin student—registering for ComSSA will grant you access to this building, regardless of your choice of degree.

If you are not doing a BEng degree and you aren't ComSSA member, you will not have access to the Engineering Pavilion, which is where ComSSA members meet on a daily basis.

Pool table

Our pool table is located in the ground floor of the Engineering Pavilion, open to use by anyone for $1 a game. Read more?

DreamSpark

DreamSpark is a Microsoft program to provide students a wide range of Microsoft software (such as Windows and Visual Studio) at no charge. ComSSA manages a DreamSpark Premium subscription on behalf of the Department of Computing, so we're the people you'll want to talk to if you want to get your hands on a bunch of free Microsoft software. Read more?

Shell accounts

Members are eligible to receive a free shell account on our Linux servers to run mission-critical applications like cmatrix and cowsay.

There are no guarantees, nor is there an SLA.

Email accounts

Anyone with a shell account also receives a basic email service at $user@comssa.org.au by Exim with access to mutt and optional forwarding via the ~/.forward file.

Web hosting

Static web hosting is available to members with shell accounts, published at http://users.comssa.org.au/$user for all to <blink> flame on </marquee>.

Unit materials, iLectures and past exams

Some of our members have archived unit media distributed via Blackboard and Echo360. Most of these units are in the departments of Computing or Mathematics. While not all units are available, we're always happy to share what we have with our members for no profit.

IRC

ComSSA provides an IRC channel for ComSSA members to talk to each-other in real-time! If you have an IRC client, join our network! If you don't, we have a web chat, and a page telling you how to connect using your own client if you so desire.
