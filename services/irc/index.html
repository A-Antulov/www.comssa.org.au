What is IRC?

IRC stands for Internet Relay Chat. IRC allows for decentralised real-time group chat. The Internet is populated with IRC networks, which are comprised of a collection of IRC servers; IRC networks have IRC channels, and you can join an IRC channel to talk to other people!

Use our web client

ComSSA provides a clean and easy-to-use web client for people that are new to IRC. Simply direct your browser to our web client, and it should connect to the ComSSA IRC for you. Be warned, you'll disconnect when you close the browser, so it's a good idea to use your own client if you plan on visiting often.

Use your own client

It is recommended that you use your own IRC client, as it makes IRC a lot easier to use, and native IRC clients are a lot easier to customise to fit your needs.

If you already have an IRC client set up, here is our server and channel information:

  • Server: irc.comssa.org.au
  • Port: 6667 (default)
  • Channel: #ComSSA

IRC clients are available on all popular platforms, and even some of the not-so-popular ones. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Windows: mIRC, Hexchat, Quassel IRC
  • OS X: Textual, Colloquy, Linkinus
  • Linux: XChat, Quassel IRC
  • Linux (command line): irssi, weechat
  • iOS: Colloquy, Limechat
  • Android: AndroIRC, Andchat

Once you have a client installed, click this link to go straight to the ComSSA channel. If not, use the connection information provided above to add a new server; if your client doesn't provide a facility to choose a channel to join, type /join #comssa into the message box.
